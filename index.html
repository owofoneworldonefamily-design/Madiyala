Our Vision

Higher Education Comes Home to Madiyala

Every year, bright students from Madiyala and its surrounding villages finish their schooling — and then stop. The nearest degree colleges are hours away, and for many families the cost of hostels, travel and fees puts higher education permanently out of reach. Anna believed no child should have to choose between their home and their future.

Anna believed no child should be deprived of education.

To understand the significance of this homecoming, we look back at a century of quiet, unstoppable growth. What began as a simple idea of Loka Seva Vrinda in this village has turned into one of the largest free education movements — One World One Family — in the country as well as abroad.

1927 → 2026

From a Single Seed to the Mighty Mission

The power of a pure and selfless thought.

1927

The Seed is Born

Sri Madiyala Narayana Bhat is born in Madiyala. His simple upbringing and deep spiritual values shape his lifelong belief that education is a basic human right, not a business.

1950

A Calling Discovered

At just 23, Narayana Bhat takes charge as Headmaster of a high school in Dakshina Kannada. The experience convinces him that real education cannot be a business — it must be an act of service.

1960–1963

Loka Seva Vrinda Begins & Alike Campus Established

Driven to serve the nation, Anna gathers a dedicated band of workers — the Tyagajeevi — and forms Loka Seva Vrinda in 1960, dedicating their lives to rural upliftment. The organisation is formally launched, and on 6 June 1963, his mother, Smt. Gouramma, hands over the primary school in Alike to Loka Seva Vrinda, establishing the first major campus.

1967–1972

Expansion of Alike Campus

The Alike campus grows significantly, marked by the inauguration of the Loka Seva Junior College.

1972

Muddenahalli Campus & Divine Assurance

The mission expands to a second location in Muddenahalli. Bhagavan Sri Sathya Sai Baba blesses the construction of the school building there and resolves their financial worries with a profound reassurance: "Shaale madidare saala hoguttade" (ಶಾಲೆ ಮಾಡಿದರೆ ಸಾಲ ಹೋಗುತ್ತದೆ) — "If you build a school, the debt will go away." He instructs Anna's team to build this second school in Muddenahalli, promising that all debts would be taken care of.

1977

A Letter of Solace

As news of Narayana Bhat's passing reaches Puttaparthi, Bhagavan Sri Sathya Sai Baba writes personally to the grieving family — words of comfort, and an assurance that the mission Anna had begun would not be allowed to falter.

1977

Consoling a Mother's Grief

Baba goes further still, personally consoling Anna's mother, Smt. Gouramma, in her sorrow — a closeness that binds the family, and the institutions they built together, ever more firmly to him.

1977

Sri Sathya Sai Loka Seva Trust

Following Narayana Bhat's passing, the organisation is renamed Sri Sathya Sai Loka Seva Trust. Baba signs to affirm the transition, ensuring the continued operation of both campuses.

2012–2023

Higher Education Expansion & University Recognition

The vision expands into higher education with the Sri Sathya Sai University for Human Excellence in Gulbarga (Kalaburagi). New campuses follow across the region through the decade, and in 2018 the Government of Karnataka grants official university status — opening the way for free undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programmes.

2023

World's First Free Medical College

Under the University's umbrella, the Sri Madhusudan Sai Institute of Medical Sciences and Research (SMSIMSR) is established in Muddenahalli. Inaugurated by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on 25 March 2023, it stands as the world's first completely free medical college, training doctors to serve the needy and underserved with absolutely no tuition fees.

2026 · Upcoming this November

A Century and Still Batting! — The Madiyala Campus

To mark the 100th birth anniversary of founder Sri Madiyala Narayana Bhat (Anna) on 30 November 2026, the mission comes full circle to his native soil. The Sri Sathya Sai University for Human Excellence is establishing a brand-new campus in Madiyala/Alike alongside a free public healthcare facility — offering modern, degree-level education, including a Bachelors in Computational Sciences and Artificial Intelligence, and equipping rural youth with technological mastery, completely free of cost.

Why Now — The Need

Why a Free B.Sc. in Computational Sciences & Artificial Intelligence Is the Need of the Hour

In the villages around Madiyala, talented young people finish their 12th standard (PUC) every summer. But as we look at the world today, technology is changing at a breathtaking pace. Computer science, software, and artificial intelligence are reshaping every single field — from farming and banking to healthcare and education. If our rural youth do not get deep, practical training in computer science right now, they will be left behind. Today, the numbers tell a harsh story for village students:

The Travel Barrier
To get a university science degree today, students from this area must travel 20 to 40 kilometers every day on public buses.
The Safety Barrier
Late-evening bus rides and dark commutes force many families — especially those with young women — to pull their children out of college entirely.
The Cost Barrier
Between college tuition, city hostel fees, and daily transport, a modern technology degree is simply out of reach for most farming and rural households.

The Solution: A 100% Free Bachelors in Computational Sciences and Artificial Intelligence. We are addressing this reality directly by launching a three-year Bachelors in Computational Sciences and Artificial Intelligence, offered completely free of charge. No tuition fees, no hidden costs. We are placing high-level programming, digital skills, and pure science education within walking distance of home — ensuring our youth can master modern technology and build strong careers without placing a financial burden on their families.

The Capital Budget Breakdown (Phase 1)

We believe in complete transparency with our benefactors. Building a permanent, university-level campus requires a direct capital investment. Phase 1 covers the core shell and structural infrastructure, with the ground floor targeted for completion by November 2026 — ready for Anna's 100th birth anniversary. The remaining floors of the core structure follow, with interiors, lab equipment, and fit-out in subsequent phases.
